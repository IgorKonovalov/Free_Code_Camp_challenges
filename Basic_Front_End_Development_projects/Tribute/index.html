Robert M. Pirsig

Dynamic Quality cannot be defined. It can only be understood intellectually through the use of analogy.

Here's a timeline of Pirsig's Life

  • 1928 - born in Minneapolis, Minnesota
  • 1943 - awarded a high school diploma and entered the University of Minnesota
  • 1946 - enlisted in the United States Army
  • 1950 - earned a bachelor of arts in Eastern Philosophy
  • 1950 - attended Banaras Hindu University in India
  • 1958 - became a professor at Montana State University in Bozeman, and taught creative writing courses for two years
  • 1961 - 1963 - suffered a nervous breakdown and spent time in and out of psychiatric clinics, treated with electroconvulsive therapy
  • 1974 - published "Zen and the Art of Motorcycle Maintenance"
  • 1974 - awarded a Guggenheim Fellowship
  • 1991 - write and published "Lila: An Inquiry into Morals"

The place to improve the world is first in one's own heart and head and hands, and then work outward from there.
